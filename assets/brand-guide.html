The entire palette sits on a warm hue axis (67°–85° for neutrals, shifting to 71° for amber). This shared warmth is why the colors feel cohesive rather than arbitrary. Moss and clay provide the semantic range (success/error) while staying within a natural earth-tone family. The neutrals form a perceptually even ramp from L=0.16 (night) to L=0.93 (linen).

Usage examples
/* Tailwind 4 utility classes */ <div class="bg-night text-sand"> <span class="text-amber font-display">krav</span> <p class="text-stone font-mono">v0.1.0</p> /* Status indicators */ <span class="text-moss">✓ passed</span> <span class="text-clay">✗ failed</span> <span class="text-amber">⚠ warning</span>
OKLCH is the native color space for Tailwind 4 and modern CSS. These values produce identical results to the hex fallbacks in all browsers that support oklch() (97%+ as of early 2026). The semantic aliases mean you write text-brand instead of text-amber, so the accent color can change without touching markup.

Both themes use the same semantic token names, just remapped. Dark: night bg + sand text. Light: linen bg + bark text. Amber, moss, and clay adjust slightly in lightness for light-mode readability (darker variants for better contrast against the linen background). The surface color (bark in dark, slightly darker linen in light) provides the card/panel differentiation layer.

Typography

Sora · DM Sans · JetBrains Mono

Display heading
Body text in DM Sans. Clean and readable for documentation, descriptions, and longer-form content. Pairs with Sora's geometric structure without competing.

Sora is the display and wordmark face — geometric skeleton with optical corrections that give it human warmth. DM Sans handles body copy. JetBrains Mono for code, CLI output, and technical labels. All three are Google Fonts, free and widely available. The letter-spacing on the wordmark is 3px at display sizes; tighten to 1-2px below 20px.

All raster assets are generated at exact pixel sizes — no upscaling. The OG images and banners use DejaVu Sans as a placeholder for Sora (which isn't available in the generation environment).
